Top Narrative Films To Study

The List of Films That Are Regularly Discussed in Film School

by Pitt Munir

CITIZEN KANE (1941)

Director

Orson Welles

Writers

Orson welles
Herman J. Mankiewicz

Cast

Orson welles
Joseph Cotten
Dorothy Comingore

The story is picturing a group of reporters are trying to decipher the last word ever spoken by Charles Foster Kane, the millionaire newspaper tycoon: "Rosebud." The film begins with a news reel detailing Kane's life for the masses, and then from there, viewers are shown flashbacks from Kane's life. As the reporters investigate further, the viewers see a display of a fascinating man's rise to fame, and how he eventually fell off the top of the world.

Citizen Kane is regularly discussed in most of film school in the US because it is considered as one of the greatest Film ever made in history of cinema. The remarkable direction of Orson Welles is making the film stand out among others to be observed especially if you are pursuing to be a director. One of topics that are frequently discussed is the mise en scene, especially the composition of the shot in earlier part of the film where the mother finally give the decision for Kane to move into the City.

STAR WARS (1977-1983)

Director

George Lucas

Writer

George Lucas

Cast

Mark Hamill
Harrison Ford
Carrie Fisher

The Epic Journey of Luke Skywalker to save the galaxy from the tyranny of Imperial possesion. Luke is just an orphan boy raised by his farmers uncle and aunt but his dream to join The Rebellion to fight The Imperial is beyond compare. His desire is so powerful that as if could manipulate the universe to help him become the hero. From Obi Wan Kenobi to Master Yoda are mentoring him to be the new hope of The Republic to rule the galaxies.

The first trilogy of one might say the original trilogy of Star Wars is one of the most common films to be discussed in film school. The simplicity of plots yet very complex story structures enriched with the outstanding characters' arch throughout the trilogy, make the films are the best example of how to write a great story for cinema.

APOCALYPSE NOW (1979)

Director

Francis Ford Coppola

Writers

John Milius
Francis Ford Coppola

Cast

Marlon Brando
Martin Sheen
Robert Duvall

It is the height of the war in Vietnam, and U.S. Army Captain Willard is sent by Colonel Lucas and a General to carry out a mission that, officially, 'does not exist - nor will it ever exist'. The mission: To seek out a mysterious Green Beret Colonel, Walter Kurtz, whose army has crossed the border into Cambodia and is conducting hit-and-run missions against the Viet Cong and NVA. The army believes Kurtz has gone completely insane and Willard's job is to eliminate him.

Aside from to be claimed as one of the greatest film in history, this Vietnam Warera film is featured in a book entitled In The Blink of An Eye by Walter Murch. The book is one of the fundamental text book for film editing. Most of film school use the book for their editing class. The author, Walter Murch is the editor of the film himself. So, if you are into film editing, Apocalypse Now is a-must-watch movie for you.
